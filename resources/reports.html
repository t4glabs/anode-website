Annual report card prepared for one of the Action research panchayats

 The annual report card is a summary of the monthly plans and achivements of Dibburhalli, one of the two GPs engaged in the action research project. The report card comprises Planned activities, Process metric to measure progress, baseline, target and achievements against plan and budgets. An assessment of the Status of Panchayat Raj Institutions in Jharkhand and Madhya Pradesh, commissioned by Pradan

The report was an outcome of a preliminary study commissioned by Pradan, to explore the scope of interventions towards strengthening rural local governance in the states of Jharkhand and Madhya Pradesh. It comprises an overview of the status of Devolution in India, based on literature survey. As per the Devolution Index report, 2015, TISS and Union Ministry of Panchayat Raj, the report states the status of the two states under study, Madhya Pradesh being somewhere in the middle (9 out of 25) while Jharkhand has been a poor performing state. The report dwells into a deeper analysis of Panchayat Raj Institutions and citizen engagement based on field visits and discussions with multiple stakeholders. The focus of the study has been to understand the implications of the legal framework to the extent they help understand the potential to work with panchayats, as well as a preliminary understanding of status on ground. Initial recommendations for way forward are included in the report.

Legal Framework and Status of Devolution in Jharkhand

The report was an outcome of a research study commissioned by Pradan in April 2016, to understand the devolution status of transferred subjects in Jharkhand. The report dwells into deeper analysis of the Jharkhand Panchayat Raj (JPR) Act, 2001 and corresponding schemes, rules and notifications relating to devolution to panchayats.  This primary research was conducted in five GPs in Basia block, Gumla district, that were identified along with Pradan and where the organisation development interventions in partnership with GPs will further be piloted
